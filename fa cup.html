Honours & Record

  • FA Cups Won: 13 (Record)
  • Total Finals: 21
  • First FA Cup Win: 1909
  • Latest FA Cup Win: 2024
  • Most Successful Manager: Sir Alex Ferguson (5 wins)

1908–09: The First Triumph

Under the guidance of Secretary-Manager Ernest Mangnall, United claimed the club's first FA Cup. Captain Charlie Roberts led a determined side to a 1–0 victory over Bristol City at the Crystal Palace stadium. Sandy Turnbull scored the historic winner, securing United's place among English football's elite and laying a foundation for future success.

1947–48: Busby's First Glory

In Matt Busby's first full season as manager, United delivered a thrilling performance at Wembley. Captained by the versatile Johnny Carey, the team overcame Stanley Matthews' Blackpool 4–2 in a classic final. This victory, United's first major trophy in nearly 40 years, announced the beginning of the iconic "Busby Babes" era and signalled the club's post-war resurgence.

1962–63: Rising from the Ashes

Five years after the Munich Air Disaster, Matt Busby completed the club's emotional rebuilding by winning the FA Cup. With Noel Cantwell as captain, United defeated Leicester City 3–1 at Wembley. Two goals from David Herd and one from Denis Law secured the trophy, providing a cathartic moment of triumph and signalling United's return to the pinnacle of the game.

1976–77: Denying the Reds

In a fiery clash against arch-rivals Liverpool, Tommy Docherty's vibrant United side pulled off a memorable upset. Captain Martin Buchan led the team to a 2–1 victory, with goals from Stuart Pearson and Jimmy Greenhoff. The win denied Bob Paisley's Liverpool, who had just won the league, a historic treble, and remains a cherished victory for United fans.

1982–83: The Replay Rout

After a stunning 2–2 draw in which Brighton's Gordon Smith missed a last-minute chance to win it, United needed a replay. Under Ron Atkinson, captain Ray Wilkins inspired a ruthless performance at Wembley. United won 4–0, with teenager Norman Whiteside becoming the youngest-ever scorer in an FA Cup final, cementing his place in club folklore.

1984–85: Whiteside's Wembley Winner

In a tense and physical final against Howard Kendall's excellent Everton side, Ron Atkinson's United triumphed again. Captain Bryan Robson lifted the cup after a 1–0 extra-time victory. The only goal came from Norman Whiteside, who curled a magnificent shot past Neville Southall, showcasing his unique talent for the big occasion.

1989–90: The Save & The Saviour

This final is etched in history as the one that saved Alex Ferguson's tenure. After a dramatic 3–3 draw with Crystal Palace, a replay was required. With captain Bryan Robson injured, goalkeeper Les Sealey and defender Lee Martin became unlikely heroes. Martin's volley secured a 1-0 win, providing the platform for the unprecedented success that would follow.

1993–94: The Double Double

As United powered towards their first league and cup "Double" of the Premier League era, they met Chelsea in the final. Captain Steve Bruce led a dominant display at Wembley. Goals from Eric Cantona (two penalties), Mark Hughes, and Brian McClair sealed a comprehensive 4-0 victory, a statement of supremacy from Sir Alex Ferguson's first great team.

1995–96: The King's Final

In a fiercely contested all-red final against Liverpool, a moment of pure genius decided the match. With the score 0-0 and time running out, United won a corner. The clearance fell to Eric Cantona on the edge of the box, and the Frenchman lashed a sublime volley through a crowded penalty area. Captain Steve Bruce lifted the cup after the 1-0 win, completing a second domestic double.

1998–99: The Treble Season

As part of the unforgettable, historic Treble, United faced Newcastle United at the old Wembley. With Peter Schmeichel captaining the side in Roy Keane's suspension-enforced absence, goals from Teddy Sheringham and Paul Scholes secured a comfortable 2-0 victory. This was the second leg of the unprecedented Premier League, FA Cup, and UEFA Champions League triumph.

2003–04: Millwall Masterclass

Held at Cardiff's Millennium Stadium while the new Wembley was built, this final was a showcase for United's attacking prowess against First Division Millwall. Captain Roy Keane lifted the trophy after a 3-0 win, with a double from Ruud van Nistelrooy and a goal from Cristiano Ronaldo, who scored his first major goal for the club in the process.

2015–16: Lingard's Lightning Strike

Louis van Gaal's tenure culminated in a dramatic FA Cup win. Captain Wayne Rooney orchestrated the play as United fought back from a goal down against Crystal Palace. After Juan Mata's equaliser, the game went to extra time. Just three minutes from a penalty shootout, Jesse Lingard unleashed an unstoppable volley to win the cup 2-1, delivering a memorable farewell for the manager.

2023–24: The Derby Defiance

In the first-ever all-Manchester FA Cup final, United entered as clear underdogs against the dominant Premier League champions. However, under immense pressure, manager Erik ten Hag masterminded a perfect tactical performance. First-half goals from teenagers Alejandro Garnacho and Kobbie Mainoo shocked City. A late City reply created tension, but captain Bruno Fernandes led a resilient defensive effort to secure a famous 2-1 victory, claiming a record-extending 13th FA Cup.

Key Records & Statistics

  • Record Final Appearances: Ryan Giggs, Roy Keane (7 each).
  • Record Final Wins: Ryan Giggs, Roy Keane (4 each).
  • Top FA Cup Scorer (All-Time for Club): Wayne Rooney, Ruud van Nistelrooy, Mark Hughes, Denis Viollet, Paul Scholes, Ryan Giggs (All tied on 20+ goals).
  • Biggest FA Cup Win: 8–0 vs Yeovil Town (1949), 8–0 vs Wrexham (1990).
  • Most FA Cup Wins by a Player: Ryan Giggs (4: 1994, 1996, 1999, 2004).
  • Part of "The Treble": 1999.

Iconic FA Cup Moments

  • 1990: Mark Robins' winner at Nottingham Forest in the third round is credited with saving Alex Ferguson's job and starting United's modern era of success.
  • 1994: Winning the first "Double Double" (league and cup in consecutive seasons).
  • 1999: The famous 2–1 semi-final replay victory over Arsenal at Villa Park, featuring Ryan Giggs' incredible solo goal and Peter Schmeichel's penalty save.
  • 2016: Marcus Rashford's debut brace in the 5–1 win against Midtjylland, followed by a winner against Arsenal, announcing his arrival.
  • 2024: Defeating rivals Manchester City 2–1 at Wembley to win the club's 13th FA Cup, securing European football and ending the season with silverware.
