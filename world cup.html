Manchester United, one of the world's most famous football clubs, has competed in the FIFA Club World Cup on one occasion. Their participation came as a result of winning the UEFA Champions League in the 2007-08 season.

2008 FIFA Club World Cup (Japan)

Tournament Results

  • Quarter-Final: Bye (as UEFA Champions League winner)
  • Semi-Final: Manchester United 5-3 Gamba Osaka (UK Time: 10:30 GMT, 19 Dec 2008) - Scorers: Vidic (28'), Ronaldo (45'), Fletcher (78'), Rooney (79', 90+1')
  • Final: Manchester United 1-0 LDU Quito (UK Time: 10:30 GMT, 21 Dec 2008) - Scorer: Wayne Rooney (73')

Key Details & Achievement

  • Venue: International Stadium, Yokohama, Japan.
  • Manager: Sir Alex Ferguson.
  • Squad: Featured stars like Wayne Rooney, Cristiano Ronaldo (FIFA Ballon d'Or winner that year), Rio Ferdinand, and Paul Scholes.
  • Tournament Winner: Manchester United were crowned FIFA Club World Cup Champions in 2008.
  • This victory completed a unique "world and European double," holding both the UEFA Champions League and FIFA Club World Cup titles simultaneously.

Club World Cup Record

  • Appearances: 1 (2008)
  • Titles: 1 (2008)
  • Total Matches: 2
  • Wins: 2
  • Goals Scored: 6
  • Top Scorer: Wayne Rooney (3 goals)

Historical Significance

The 2008 Club World Cup win cemented Manchester United's status as the best club side in the world at that time. It remains the only time the club has won the global tournament, adding a prestigious world title to its extensive trophy cabinet.

Honours

  • FIFA Club World Cup Champions: 2008
  • UEFA Champions League Winners (Qualifying Title): 2007-08

Legacy

Manchester United's name is engraved on the FIFA Club World Cup trophy alongside other global giants. The victory in Yokohama is remembered as a pivotal moment in the club's modern history under Sir Alex Ferguson, marking its dominance on the world stage.
